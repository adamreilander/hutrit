# Campaign Report — Hutrit / Meta Ads

## Report Period
From 2026-02-28 to 2026-03-29

## Platform
Meta Ads

## Primary Objective
Generate landing page visits for talent attraction and related campaigns.

## Main Result Type
Landing page visits

## General Performance Summary
During this period, the campaigns generated a total of:

- **17,319 landing page visits**
- **€277.99 total spend**
- **414,747 impressions**
- **260,942 reach**

## Overall Interpretation
The account shows strong efficiency in traffic generation at a relatively low cost per landing page visit across multiple campaigns.

There is clear evidence that some campaigns significantly outperformed others, especially in terms of cost efficiency and volume, while a few campaigns consumed budget with lower efficiency.

The report suggests that the advertising system found strong traction in campaigns related to Sales Development and some role-specific talent campaigns.

## Performance Patterns Observed

### 1. Sales-related campaigns performed best
The strongest traffic efficiency came from campaigns tied to Sales Development and commercial roles.

### 2. Tech campaigns showed solid potential
Campaigns related to tech and AI performed reasonably well and could be worth further scaling or refinement.

### 3. Paid Media underperformed in efficiency
Although Paid Media generated a good number of visits, its cost per result was much higher than the rest. This may indicate weaker targeting, a less resonant message, or a more competitive audience.

### 4. Budget was not always aligned with efficiency
Some campaigns with excellent cost efficiency had relatively low spend, while a weaker campaign like Paid Media absorbed the highest spend in the report.

## Optimization Recommendations

### Budget Allocation
- Increase investment in campaigns with proven low cost per result
- Prioritize validated winners before scaling weaker campaigns
- Reallocate budget away from underperforming traffic campaigns unless they serve a strategic test purpose

### Campaign Replication
Use top-performing campaigns as references for:
- audience targeting
- messaging
- role naming and framing
- creative structure
- landing page alignment

### Creative Testing
For weaker campaigns, test:
- new hooks
- clearer role-specific messaging
- stronger value proposition in the ad copy
- better visual differentiation
- different CTA structures

### Targeting Review
Review audience quality and segmentation in underperforming campaigns, especially:
- Paid Media
- Cyber
- Automation Expert
- SEO-related campaigns

### Scale Logic
When scaling, prioritize:
1. Sales Development 2
2. Director Comercial
3. Sales Development
4. Campaña IA
5. Campaña Tech nueva

## Notes for AI Agent Context
When using this campaign history as context, the agent should understand that:

- the main objective analyzed here is landing page traffic
- Sales Development campaigns are strong historical winners
- Director Comercial also showed strong traffic efficiency
- Paid Media was expensive relative to the rest
- AI and Tech campaigns show promising potential
- future recommendations should favor efficient winners first, then controlled testing for weaker segments
- traffic performance alone does not confirm lead quality, so future analysis should distinguish between cheap visits and meaningful conversions

## Strategic Summary
This report shows that the Meta Ads account has already identified strong opportunities in some audience and role categories, particularly Sales Development and commercial roles.

The next strategic step is not to treat all campaigns equally, but to:
- scale proven winners
- reduce waste from weaker campaigns
- test improved variants of underperforming segments
- connect traffic performance with actual downstream conversion quality

## Short Version
Meta Ads performance during this period was generally efficient for traffic generation. The clear top performer was Sales Development 2, followed by Director Comercial and Sales Development. Paid Media generated volume but at a much higher cost. The account should prioritize scaling proven winners and refining weaker audience segments.
